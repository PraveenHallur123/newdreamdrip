Perfect Fit Size Guide

Detailed measurement instructions for your custom DreamDrip garment

How to Measure

Get Assistance

Have someone help you for more accurate measurements, especially for hard-to-reach areas.

Wear Proper Clothing

Wear form-fitting undergarments or the type of undergarments you plan to wear with the garment.

Use Soft Tape

Use a flexible measuring tape that conforms to your body. Don't use a metal tape measure.

Stand Naturally

Stand straight with feet together, arms at sides. Don't suck in or push out.

Key Measurement Points

Bust

1. Bust/Chest

How to measure: Measure around the fullest part of your bust/chest, keeping tape parallel to the floor.

Tip: Don't pull the tape too tight. It should be snug but not compressing.

Waist

2. Waist

How to measure: Measure around the narrowest part of your natural waist (usually above the belly button).

Tip: Bend to the side to find your natural waist crease.

Hip

3. Hip

How to measure: Measure around the fullest part of your hips, usually 7-9 inches below your waist.

Tip: Include your buttocks in the measurement.

Shoulder

4. Shoulder Width

How to measure: Measure from the edge of one shoulder to the edge of the other shoulder across your back.

Tip: Keep the tape straight and level.

Sleeve

5. Sleeve Length

How to measure: From shoulder point to wrist bone, with arm slightly bent.

Tip: Measure along the outside of your arm.

Length

6. Garment Length

How to measure: From shoulder point down to desired hem length.

Tip: Measure a similar garment you already own for reference.

Fit & Style Tips

For Evening Gowns

  • Add 1-2 inches to length for heels
  • Consider undergarments when measuring
  • Think about movement and sitting comfort

For Day Dresses

  • Allow ease for movement
  • Consider seasonal layering
  • Measure with shoes you'll typically wear

For Bridal Wear

  • Schedule final fitting 1 month before event
  • Consider weight fluctuations
  • Account for any body alterations planned

General Advice

  • Measure at the same time each day
  • Update measurements every 6-12 months
  • When in doubt, measure twice
